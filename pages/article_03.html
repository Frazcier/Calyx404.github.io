From Chalkboards to Chatbots

Guiding Principles of Ethical Prompting for AI-Augmented Learners

By Raymond Agustin

Source: https://locobuzz.com/wp-content/uploads/Screenshot-2023-10-11-165128.png

Picture this: it's 11:54 PM, and that pesky essay, due in just five minutes, is as blank as your mind. Panic sets in, and then, like a knight in digital armor, ChatGPT swoops in to save the day!

"Write me an essay on the importance of coffee consumption in student productivity!" you command, and voilà—in seconds, you've gone from not-a-chance-in-hell to call-me-professor faster than you can say “deadline.”

But hold on—before you hit 'submit' without a second thought, it's time to pause and think about ethical prompting. To start, what exactly are chatbots? Simply put, they are AI programs designed to engage in conversation with users and perform tasks based on user instructions—known as prompts. From tutoring students to writing emails for marketing businesses, chatbots have quickly become part of our everyday lives. For students today, chatbots like ChatGPT serve as indispensable academic companions—providing instant help with writing, problem-solving, and research. However, while these tools can be super helpful, we must also slow down a bit and consider the ethics in using these AI chatbots.

Why does ethical prompting matters? Ethics in AI isn't just a buzzword for clout; it's a significant principle that addresses responsible AI usage, ensuring that technology enhances our learning without compromising our values or integrity. So, without further ado, let's dive into the eight guiding principles of ethical prompting so you can ace your assignments—and create a productive and respectful learning environment.

  1. Keep your data safe and private

    2. Source: https://pin.it/4GmbWiNxS

    Before you send in your prompt, consider the information you're sharing. Your chatbot buddy is fantastic, but sharing personal information is a big no-no. Think of it like meeting a stranger: you wouldn't share your entire life story right away! When interacting with a chatbot, avoid giving away sensitive details. For instance, if you're asking for help with a homework, just provide the problem without including your school's name or personal anecdotes. Keep it casual but safe!

  2. Ask the right questions for better results

    3. Source: https://pin.it/3vkaI2XUW

    Be a clever questioner! The quality of their output heavily relies on the prompts you give. Instead of vague prompts like, "Tell me about climate change", try something more specific, such as, "What are the major causes of climate change, and how does it affect agriculture?" This way, you're likely to get clearer, more useful insights.

  3. Check and correct biased output

    4. Source: https://pin.it/28jUjJ15o

    AI language models are not perfect; they can sometimes generate biased or inaccurate content. When you receive an answer, double-check it. If a chatbot presents a perspective that seems oversimplified, one-sided, or prejudiced, challenge it or seek more balanced information. Don't simply accept what it says like a gospel. For example, if you're researching historical events and the chatbot presents an off narrative, cross-reference it with reputable sources. Remember, being a critical thinker also applies when chatting with AI.

  4. Be open and take responsibility

    5. Source: https://pin.it/Qw8iUumgr

    Transparency goes a long way in ethical prompting. If you're using AI-generated content in your assignments, acknowledge it. There's no shame in seeking help, but authenticity matters. It's like giving credit where credit is due—whether human or AI. If your paper includes ideas or phrases generated by ChatGPT, acknowledge and mention that contribution. This demonstrates integrity and helps promote responsible AI chatbot use. As students, you're not just consumers of information; you're stewards of academic honesty!

  5. Be fair and avoid harm

    6. Source: https://pin.it/Qw8iUumgr

    Ethical use of chatbots also involves considering the consequences of your requests. Avoid prompts that could lead to harmful or discriminatory outputs. For example, asking for a joke at the expense of a marginalized group isn't just unethical; it's downright harmful. Aim to utilize AI to foster understanding and inclusion rather than perpetuating stereotypes and harming others. Ask yourself, “Would I be comfortable sharing this response publicly?” If not, it might be time to rethink that prompt.

  6. Maintain academic honesty

    7. Source: https://pin.it/5rdBiURJU

    Chatbots are there to assist you, not to do the work for you. Using the generated text without attribution in your paper can not only violate academic honesty, but also lead to severe consequences. So, instead of letting AI write your entire essay, think of it as a brainstorming partner—get ideas, draft outlines, or seek feedback. Maintaining academic honesty is essential in building a foundation of trust and respect within your academe.

  7. Support teachers and learn AI skills

    8. Source: https://pin.it/5QIW7enmU

    The educational landscape is evolving, and chatbots will likely be around for quite a while, so why not make the most of them by supporting your teachers? Use AI as a complement to classroom learning. For instance, open up conversations about how to ethically integrate chatbots into your coursework. Sharing your insights can empower educators to better understand your needs, and together, you can create effective guidelines for AI chatbots usage. Plus, remember this, learning how to use AI tools responsibly is a skill in itself that will serve you well in the future job market.

  8. Use AI to enhance learning and keep improving

    9. Source: https://pin.it/4iFyre2AD

    Finally, embrace AI as a supplement to enrich your learning experience. Use it to explore topics deeper, get multiple perspectives, and practice skills. For instance, if you're preparing for an exam, use AI to generate quiz questions and assess your knowledge. Think of it as an extra tool in your study kit; the more you practice, the better you become! The goal is to grow and improve throughout your academic journey.

As we've traversed through the guiding principles of ethical prompting, it's clear that responsibility lies in our hands as learners. We learned that chatbots can be valuable tools in our academic toolkit, but ethical prompting is crucial to ensuring that we use them wisely.

To recap: chatbots simulate conversation which offers instantaneous assistance that can make studying a lot less stressful. However, ethical prompting matters because it ensures we are not only smart but also respectful and responsible AI users.

So, if you find yourself staring at your pile of impending deadlines, remember these principles before reaching for that AI assistant. They will not only ease your learning experience but also help you use technology in a way that supports your growth rather than undermining it.

Let's make a conscious effort to embrace technology while keeping our values intact. So, this academic year, as you juggle projects and papers, keep AI ethical prompting at the forefront of your study habits. Get smart faster, but do it the right way! Study well, students!
